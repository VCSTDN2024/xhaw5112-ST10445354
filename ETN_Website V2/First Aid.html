First Aid

The Purpose of First Aid

First aid courses are structured training programs designed to equip individuals with the knowledge and skills required to provide immediate care to someone who is injured or experiencing a medical emergency. These courses cover a broad range of scenarios, from minor injuries to life-threatening situations, and aim to stabilize the patient until professional medical help arrives.

What Content will be covered:

  • Wounds and Bleeding
  • Burns and Fractures
  • Cardio-Pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)
  • Respiratory distress e.g. Choking, blocked airway

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) and AED Use:

  • CPR Techniques: Training on how to perform chest compressions and rescue breaths effectively.
  • AED: Instruction on how to use an automated external defibrillator, which can restore a normal heart rhythm in cases of cardiac arrest.

Bleeding Control and Wound Care:

  • Direct Pressure: How to apply pressure to stop bleeding.
  • Bandaging: Proper techniques for bandaging wounds.
  • Tourniquets: Use of tourniquets for severe bleeding.

Fractures and Sprains:

Techniques for immobilizing fractures and sprains using splints and slings.

Burns and Scalds:

First aid for different types of burns, including thermal, chemical, and electrical burns.

Choking:

  • Heimlich Maneuver: For conscious adults and children.
  • Back Blows and Chest Thrusts: For infants or unconscious patients.

Upon completing a first aid course, participants often receive a certification that is valid for a specific period, usually two to three years. After this period, a recertification course may be required to refresh and update skills. Some professions may mandate regular recertification.
